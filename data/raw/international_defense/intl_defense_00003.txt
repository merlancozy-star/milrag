UPDATED 6/16/2026 at 9:37 am ET with new information from a press conference at Edwards Air Force Base. 

WASHINGTON A US Air Force 
B-52 Stratofortress
 bomber crashed Monday after taking off from Edwards Air Force Base in California, killing eight people on board the aircraft, according to military authorities.

Col. James Hayes, deputy commander of the 412th Test Wing at Edwards, said in a briefing with reporters Monday that the crash was unsurvivable. A mixed crew of military and government contractors were aboard the bomber, according to Hayes.

Boeing, the prime contractor for the Stratofortress, said in a 
statement
 Monday evening that two company employees were on board the B-52 that crashed.

 We extend our deepest condolences to the loved ones of the eight crew members who lost their lives in the incident, the company said. 

The crash occurred around 11:20 am local time, and Hayes said the aircraft was supporting a radar modernization program for the bomber. Edwards is located in the desert north of Los Angeles.

Officials are now investigating the cause of the crash, and a subsequent accident investigation board will determine what information can be released to the public, according to Hayes. The process could take upwards of six months, he said.
