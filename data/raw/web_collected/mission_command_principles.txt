Mission Command — Core Principles and Doctrine (FM 6-0)

Mission Command is the exercise of authority and direction by the commander using mission orders to enable disciplined initiative within the commanders intent to empower agile and adaptive leaders in the conduct of unified land operations.

Six Principles of Mission Command:
1. Build cohesive teams through mutual trust
2. Create shared understanding
3. Provide a clear commanders intent
4. Exercise disciplined initiative
5. Use mission orders
6. Accept prudent risk

Commander Tasks: Understand / Visualize / Describe / Direct / Lead / Assess

Operations Process: Plan / Prepare / Execute / Assess

Staff Tasks: Conduct operations process / Knowledge and information management / Inform and influence activities / Cyber electromagnetic activities
