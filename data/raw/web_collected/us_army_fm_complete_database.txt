US Army Field Manuals — Complete Database
As of July 2007, 542 field manuals were in use. Under Doctrine 2015 (started 2010), the most important doctrinal publications were reorganized into ADP (Army Doctrine Publications), ADRP (Doctrine Reference Publications), ATP (Techniques Publications), TC (Training Circulars), and TM (Technical Manuals). 50 select FMs continue to be published.

=== CAPSTONE MANUALS ===
FM 1, The Army (14 June 2005): Establishes fundamental principles for employing landpower. Together with FM 3-0, one of two capstone doctrinal manuals.
FM 3-0, Operations (14 June 2001, updated 2011): Lays out fundamentals of war fighting. Covers Full Spectrum Operations, Mission Command, Six Warfighting Functions.
FM 5-0, The Operations Process (26 Mar 2010): Plan, Prepare, Execute, Assess cycle. Design methodology for complex problems.
FM 6-0, Mission Command (2011): Six principles — Build cohesive teams, Create shared understanding, Provide clear commander's intent, Exercise disciplined initiative, Use mission orders, Accept prudent risk.
FM 7-0, Training the Force: Battle focused training, full spectrum operations training.

=== NOTABLE MANUALS ===
FM 3-05.70 (Survival Manual, formerly FM 21-76): Comprehensive wilderness survival guide.
FM 3-24 (Counterinsurgency, 2014): Petraeus/Mattis counterinsurgency doctrine. Landmark publication that changed US military approach to COIN operations in Iraq and Afghanistan. Emphasizes population-centric approach, unity of effort, and political primacy.
FM 5-31 (Boobytraps, 1965): No longer active but still frequently referenced in EOD training.
FM 27-10 (Rules of War, 1956, last modified 1976): Cornerstone of Law of War for US military. Incorporates Geneva Conventions and customary international law.
FM 34-52 (Intelligence Interrogation, 2005): Used to train CIA interrogators. Replaced by FM 2-22.3 in Sept 2006 after Abu Ghraib scandal with revised interrogation standards.
FM 3-25.150 (Combatives): Hand-to-hand combat system based on Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu and wrestling.
FM 3-19.15 (Civil Disturbance Operations): Riot control and crowd management doctrine.
FM 3-97.61 (Military Mountaineering): Mountain and cold weather operations.
FM 3-11 series (CBRN): NBC Defense, Decontamination, Protection, Chemical/Biological agents reference.
FM 31-27 (Pack Animals in SOF): Use of mules and pack animals in special operations.

=== DOCTRINE 2015 TRANSITION ===
Old FM numbering (e.g., FM 100-5 Operations) replaced by FM 3-0 series to align with joint doctrine numbering (JP 3-0). Key ADP/ADRP publications:
ADP 1: The Army / ADP 2-0: Intelligence / ADP 3-0: Operations / ADP 4-0: Sustainment / ADP 5-0: The Operations Process / ADP 6-0: Mission Command / ADP 7-0: Training
Each ADP has a corresponding ADRP providing detailed implementation guidance.
