US Joint Publications (JP) — Complete Reference
Joint doctrine presents fundamental principles that guide the employment of US military forces in coordinated action toward a common objective. JP 1 is the capstone.

CAPSTONE:
JP 1, Doctrine for the Armed Forces of the United States: Highest-level joint doctrine.
JP 1-0, Joint Personnel Support.

JOINT OPERATIONS (JP 3 Series):
JP 3-0, Joint Operations: Foundation for all joint operations. Seven joint functions.
JP 3-01, Countering Air and Missile Threats: IADS, air defense, missile defense.
JP 3-02, Amphibious Operations: Ship-to-shore movement, landing force operations.
JP 3-03, Joint Interdiction: Disrupting enemy capabilities before they can be used.
JP 3-05, Special Operations: SOF employment across the range of military operations.
JP 3-06, Joint Urban Operations: Operations in urban environments.
JP 3-07, Stability: Post-conflict stability operations framework.
JP 3-08, Interorganizational Coordination: Whole-of-government approach.
JP 3-09, Joint Fire Support: Integration of joint fires.
JP 3-10, Joint Security Operations: Force protection and security.
JP 3-11, CBRN Environment Operations: Chemical, biological, radiological, nuclear.
JP 3-12, Cyberspace Operations: Offensive and defensive cyber.
JP 3-13, Information Operations: Influence, deception, EW, OPSEC.
JP 3-14, Space Operations: Space as a warfighting domain.
JP 3-15, Barriers and Obstacle Planning: Countermobility and breaching.
JP 3-16, Multinational Operations: Coalition and alliance operations.
JP 3-17, Air Mobility Operations: Strategic and tactical airlift.
JP 3-18, Joint Forcible Entry: Airborne, air assault, amphibious entry.
JP 3-22, Foreign Internal Defense: Supporting allied internal security.
JP 3-24, Counterinsurgency: Population-centric COIN doctrine.
JP 3-25, Countering Threat Networks: Network analysis and disruption.
JP 3-26, Counterterrorism: CT policy and operational framework.
JP 3-27, Homeland Defense: Defense of US territory and population.
JP 3-28, Defense Support of Civil Authorities: Military aid to civil power.
JP 3-29, Foreign Humanitarian Assistance: HA/DR operations.
JP 3-30, Joint Air Operations: Air component command and control.
JP 3-31, Joint Land Operations: Ground component operations.
JP 3-32, Joint Maritime Operations: Naval component operations.
JP 3-33, Joint Task Force Headquarters: JTF organization and command.
JP 3-34, Joint Engineer Operations: Engineer support to joint operations.
JP 3-35, Deployment and Redeployment Operations: Force projection.
JP 3-40, Joint Countering Weapons of Mass Destruction: CWMD framework.
JP 3-52, Joint Airspace Control: Airspace coordination and deconfliction.
JP 3-57, Civil-Military Operations: CIMIC and civil affairs.
JP 3-59, Meteorological and Oceanographic Operations: Weather and environment.
JP 3-60, Joint Targeting: Target development, engagement, assessment.
JP 3-61, Public Affairs: Media operations and strategic communication.
JP 3-63, Detainee Operations: EPW and detainee handling.
JP 3-68, Noncombatant Evacuation Operations: NEO planning and execution.
JP 3-72, Nuclear Operations: Nuclear employment and deterrence.
JP 3-80, Resource Management: Financial and resource planning.
JP 3-84, Legal Support: Law of war, ROE, legal advice.
JP 3-85, Joint Electromagnetic Spectrum Operations: EMS superiority.
