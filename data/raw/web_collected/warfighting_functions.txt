Six Warfighting Functions — US Army Doctrine (FM 3-0)

1. MISSION COMMAND: Develops and integrates activities enabling commanders to balance the art of command and the science of control.
2. MOVEMENT AND MANEUVER: Moves forces to achieve positions of advantage relative to the enemy.
3. INTELLIGENCE: Synchronizes collection, processing, analysis, and dissemination of information.
4. FIRES: Creates effects to support operations through targeting and fire support.
5. SUSTAINMENT: Provides logistics, personnel services, and health service support.
6. PROTECTION: Preserves the force through air defense, CBRN defense, security, and information protection.

Combined arms and mutual support represent the payoff of integration across all functions. These six functions, bound by leadership, replace the older Battlefield Operating Systems (BOS).
